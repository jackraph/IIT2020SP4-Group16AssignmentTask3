Comparisons

Similarities between the roles

Communication is the one skill common to all four jobs. Almost all our roles require some interaction with clients which requires communication. Communication skills are also important when working within a team. An Analyst Programmer needs to consult with the client to aid their evaluation of what needs to be done to create the requested application. An IT Help Desk employee must determine what exactly the client needs, help them troubleshoot, and find a solution to any IT issues they are having. An Optometrist must instruct their clients how to perform the required actions during an eye exam. They must also communicate their recommendations for a client’s treatment and what corrective measures they should take to aid their eyesight.

An Analyst Programmer and a Japanese Mobile App Engineer will both require programming skills in multiple languages, skills with collaborative services like GitHub and experience with agile methodology as most software creating companies now follow it. Help Desk staff will need some programming knowledge, and experience with the command line and scripting.

Differences between the roles

The Japanese Mobile App Developer has the largest Average Salary. Focusing on the Australian context, the Optometrist and Analyst Programmer roles have a similar average salary. This is reminiscent of a professional salary whereas the Help Desk average salary more closely resembles an entry level or graduate salary.

Many of our ideal jobs have overlapping skills and requirements, however, the IT industry is broad and diverse. Although the differences between certain roles may not seem immediately clear, they do exist. One programmer may need to know entirely different code languages and design methodologies from another programmer while an IT help desk employee might focus on scripting.

A Japanese Mobile App Engineer will need to speak Japanese. This is a skill that the other roles do not require in the Australian context.

An Optometrist will need special training so they may perform eye exams safely, diagnose diseases, and prescribe treatment/corrective lenses.

An IT help desk employee will focus on customer service skills more than the other roles. They will also need the ability to extract information from people who are unable to properly articulate the issues they are experiencing due to their lack of IT knowledge, similar to the Analyst Programmer.

An Analyst programmer will deal with business orientated software development. This will require a deeper understanding of a clients' business needs, their hardware limitations, and perhaps most importantly, their security requirements.
